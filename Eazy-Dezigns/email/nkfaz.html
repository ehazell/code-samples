RANDI'S STORY: THE GIFT OF LIFE
 

The holidays are usually a busy time for all of us. Last year, just a week before Christmas, Randi Ruiz got some news that stopped her holiday plans in their tracks – end stage renal failure. Doctors didn’t expect her to live more than another week, but with dialysis treatments she returned to work within two months. Despite her improvements, her life would continue to be at risk without a kidney transplant.


Randi found Bonnie Palomo, a co-worker who understood her situation. Bonnie wasn’t a match for one of her family members who needed a transplant, but was a match for Randi. Bonnie gave Randi her life back – and also allowed another to come into the world. Randi recently had a daughter, Ana Lia, a blessing that would have never been possible in her previous condition.

WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE?
 
 
 

Most people may not have any severe symptoms until their kidney disease is advanced.

However, you may notice that you:

  • feel more tired and have less energy
  • have trouble concentrating or sleeping
  • have a poor appetite
  • have muscle cramping at night or swollen feet and ankles
  • have puffiness around your eyes, especially in the morning
  • have dry, itchy skin
  • need to urinate more often, especially at night
